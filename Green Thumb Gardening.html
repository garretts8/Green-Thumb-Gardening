Indoor Gardening

Did you know that to save time during the winter season you could start your vegetable garden indoors? Have you ever wondered what it would take to grow healthy vegetables from the comfort of your home? Do you desire to have fresh greens, no matter the season, and at little to no cost to you? Keep reading to learn more about starting an indoor garden.

Decide When to Plant

First you need to start by deciding when you want to plant. If you intend on just growing herbs indoors or you are planning on growing and maintaining an indoor garden you can begin right away. If your intention is to grow an indoor garden, and to later transfer your indoor garden outdoors, then you might want to start your garden a little closer to spring. You will also want to pay close attention to the weather. Do not plant outside until you are sure that daytime and nighttime temperatures will remain above 45 degrees Fahrenheit for a growing season, otherwise the startling might die or their growth may be stunted.

Choose a Location

You will need to have a sunny window (preferably a south facing window). If a sunny window is not available you also have the option of using grow lights. AeroGarden and Hydroponics have growing systems that are great for starting and maintaining an indoor garden, and they can be used in any area of the house, so long as the temperature can be maintained at 65 degrees or higher at night, and 70 – 80 degrees during the day.

Decide What to Plant

Microgreen gardens

If you are planting a microgreen garden then you could be growing and harvesting vegetables in as little as 7 to 10 days. Turnips, chives, nasturtiums, sunflowers, wheatgrass, peas, salad mix, alliums and more can be planted in a microgreen garden. You will need a lot of seeds to do a microgreen garden. Bulk seeds can be purchased at places like Leaf Market or Everwilde Farms. Dependent on the type of seeds that you wish to plant, you might need to prepare the seeds by soaking them for 2 to 3 days before planting them. After preparing the seed, begin by adding 2 cups of water to a 10 X 20 starter tray. Add soil until it is just below the rim, and smooth out the surface. Sprinkle ¼ cup of seed evenly over the top of the soil. Fill a spray bottle with water and spray a layer of mist over the seed mixture. Cover your tray using another 10 X 20 tray, and check in 2 to 3 days to see if your seeds have germinated. When the seeds have begun to grow uncover the seedlings and turn on your growing lights. Fluorescent and LED growing lights work best, and should be used 12 to 16 hours a day only. Any longer than that and you could burn your plants. When your plants have grown their first pair of true leaves(not your seed leaves) then is the time for your garden to be harvested. Grasses can be cut and allowed to regrow up to three times before you would have to begin a new batch.

Growing Other Vegetables of a Longer Duration

You can also plant vegetables like carrots, lettuce, peas, potatoes, sweet potatoes, radishes, among other things. The sky is the limit if you have enough space and proper lighting. You can grow your indoor garden in a large container with holes in the bottom or you can use peat pellets in a mini greenhouse. For those using peat pellets, follow the instructions given in the greenhouse packet. If you are planting in a larger container then you’ll need to place one to two inches of organic potting soil into your container. Scatter seeds in rows over the surface of the soil. You'll then want to cover your seeds with a thin layer of soil. Place the container with your seeds into a drip tray, and then gently water your seeds. Place saran wrap over the top of the tray and poke a few holes in it. Allow for your seeds to grow. Once leaves begin to appear, remove the saran wrap and place the tray under your grow light. Follow direction on your seed packet to determine how often your particular vegetable would need to be watered and how much light is required. You might find that you want certain vegetables directly under the light, while others would prefer to be placed a little further away from the light. As your plants grow, pay close attention to their roots. If the container is too small the plant can become root bound, which can stunt its growth and affect how quickly it will start producing. When space for the roots become limited transplant the plant in a larger container.

Be Consistent

Create a habit of watering your plant at the same time. Consistency helps the plants to stay out of survival mode, and will allow your plant to grow and produce. Enjoy watching your garden grow, and relish in the blessing of having fresh, healthy, delicious vegetables right at your fingertips.
