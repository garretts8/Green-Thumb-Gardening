Overview

Purpose

The goal of the website is to share gardening tips on how to start an indoor vegetable garden. The website provides planting techniques specific to certain plants, and lets you know how quickly you can harvest certain vegetables. Did you know that to save time and money you could start your garden indoors? Want to get a jump on the season and save weeks of growing time? Do you desire to have fresh greens, no matter the season, and at little to no cost to you? Keep reading to discover how easy it is to start your garden today!

Audience

My audience would be people who simply desire to learn how to garden indoors. It would attract people who live in urban communities with limited to no outdoor space and who desire to learn how to grow and maintain an indoor garden. The website will also attract those who live in colder climates with shorter growing seasons who wish to shave off a few weeks off of their growing season. And it will provide an opportunity to grow a variety of vegetables that are not typically found as starter plants in stores.

Indoor Gardening

Did you know that to save time during the winter season you could start your vegetable garden indoors? Have you ever wondered what it would take to grow healthy vegetables from the comfort of your home? Read on to learn more about starting an indoor garden.

Decide When to Plant

First you need to start by deciding what you want to plant. If you intend on just growing herbs indoors or you are planning on growing and maintaining an indoor garden you can begin right away. If you intention is to grow an indoor garden, and to later transfer your indoor garden outdoors, then you might want to start your garden a little closer to spring. You will also want to pay close attention to the weather. Do not plant outside until you are sure that daytime and nighttime temperatures will remain above 45 degrees Fahrenheit for a growing season, otherwise the startling will die or its growth may be stunted.

Choose a good location

You would need to have a sunny window (preferably a south facing window). If a sunny windo is not available you also have the option of using grow lights. AeroGarden and Hydroponics have growing systems that are great for starting and maintaining an indoor garden, and they can be used in any area of the house, so long as the temperature can be maintained at 65 degrees or higher at night, and 70 – 80 degrees during the day.

Decide What to Plant

Microgreen gardens

If you are planting a microgreen garden then you could be growing and harvesting vegetables in as little as 7 to 10 days. Turnips, chives, nasturtiums, sunflowers, wheatgrass, peas, salad mix, chives, alliums and more can be planted in a microgreen garden. You will need a lot of seeds to do a microgreen garden. Bulk seeds can be purchased at places like Leaf Market or Everwilde Farms. Dependent on the type of seed that you want to plant, you might need to soak your seed for 2 to 3 days before planting them. Begin by adding 2 cups of water to your 10 X 20 starter tray. Add soil until it is just below the rim, and smooth out the surface. Sprinkle ¼ cup of seed evenly over the top of the soil. Fill a spray bottle with water and spray a layer of mist over the seed mixture. Cover your tray using another 10 X 20 tray, and check in 2 to three days to if your seeds have germinated. When the seeds have begun to grow uncover the seedlings and turn on your growing lights. Fluorescent and LED growing lights work best, and should be used 12 to 16 hours a day only. Any longer than that and you could burn your plants. When your plants have grown their first pair of true leaves (not your seed leaves) then it is time for your garden to be harvested. Grasses can be cut and allowed to regrow up to three times before you would have to begin a new batch.

Growing Other Vegetables of a Longer Duration

You can also plant vegetables like carrots, lettuce, peas, potatoes, sweet potatoes, radishes, among other things. The sky is the limit if you have enough space and proper lighting. You can grow your indoor garden in a large container with holes in the bottom for or you can use peat pellets in a mini green house. For those using peat pellets, follow the instructions given in the greenhouse packet. If you are planting in a larger container then you’ll need to place one to two inches of organic potting soil into your container. Scatter seeds in rows over the surface of the soil. You'll then want to cover your seeds with a thin layer of soil. Place the container with your seeds into a drip tray, and then gently water your seeds. Place saran wrap over the top of the tray and poke a few holes on the top. Allow for your seeds to grow. Once leaves begin to appear remove the saran wrap and place the tray under your grow light. Follow direction on your seed packet to determine how often your particular vegetable would need to be watered and how much light is required. You might find that you want certain vegetables directly under the light, while others would prefer to be placed a little further away. As your plants grow, pay close attention to their roots as some vegetables might require that they be placed in larger containers as they grow in order for their growth to not be stunted, and so that the vegetable might produce.

Be Consistent

Create a habit of watering your plant consistently at the same time, this helps the plant from staying out of survival mode, and will allow your plant to grow and produce. Enjoy watching your garden grow, and enjoy the blessing of having fresh, healthy, delicious vegetables right at your fingertips.

Planting an Outdoor garden

If you want to just get a jump start on growing your garden, you can start your garden indoors. Even when it is cold outside you could be preparing for the time when you will be able to move your indoor garden outside. Rather than pay exorbitant prices in having others do the work for you, you could raise your own starter plants and save.

When do I Plant Outdoors?

Once you have your garden up and growing, and temperatures outside have risen to 50 degrees or higher at night and even higher during the day, once there is no risk of your plant becoming frost bitten, you can start to acclimate your plants. You can do this by taking your plants outside for 2 to 4 hours for a day or two, then gradually increasing the number of hours until you can leave them outside the entire time without fear of the plant being frostbitten or stunted. When this happens, it is time to plant your indoor garden outside.

Tips on Transferring My Garden

Every vegetable has different requirements to get the most yield. Read your seed packet to discover what are the requirements for your special brand of seeds. For the most part tomatoes should be planted deep into the dirt, covering the stems up to the first set of branches, and then watering them well to avoid the plant going into shock. Tomato plants typically should be planted 18 to 24 inches apart. Cucumbers, melons, pumpkins, squash, and zucchini grow best when you build the dirt up in the shape of a mound. Plant your vines at the top of the mound, and water well. Cucumbers should be planted 3 to 4 feet apart, pumpkins 6 to 8 feet apart. Squash, zucchini and melons require a lot more space. They require as much as 18 to 30 feet apart dependent on the particular type of species. Only use fertilizers safe for use on vegetables. Pay attention to watering details on your seed packet to avoid over watering or burning your plant.

A Healthier Choice

With a bit of planning, you and your family could enjoy freshly grown vegetables right from your very own garden. This could help in teaching family members necessary self-reliance skills, improve their general health, and could be a great advocate of promoting healthy lifestyles.
